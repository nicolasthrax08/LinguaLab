# LinguaLab — Demo Script (3 Minutes)

**Goal:** Show the problem, the user, the live product, and the responsible AI features in under 3 minutes.

---

## 0:00–0:20 — Problem & Hook

**Visual:** Stressed student staring at a professor’s door; phone in hand.

**Voiceover:**
> “Millions of brilliant international students struggle not with ideas, but with *how to express them* in unfamiliar academic cultures. They freeze in office hours, sound too direct in emails, or get labeled ‘quiet’ when they’re actually reading the room.”

---

## 0:20–0:35 — User & Context

**Visual:** Fictional persona screen: “Mei, from Shenzhen, applying to a U.S. internship.”

**Voiceover:**
> “Meet Mei. She’s top of her class. But before every professor meeting, she rehearses in her head for ten minutes. She’s not alone—language-barrier stress affects the majority of international students.”

---

## 0:35–1:50 — Live Walkthrough

**Screen 1: Landing**
- Show value prop: “Practice academic conversations with AI feedback.”

**Screen 2: Onboarding**
- Mei selects English variety: “Indian English” (or “General American”).
- Selects proficiency: “Intermediate.”
- Chooses scenario: “Office Hours.”

**Screen 3: Practice Chat**
- Mei types: “Professor, I need more time for the assignment.”
- AI (as professor) replies: “What’s the reason? When can you submit it?”
- Mei continues: “I have two exams this week. I can submit it by Friday.”

**Screen 4: Feedback Panel**
- Show three feedback cards:
  - Language: “'Need more time' → 'I was wondering if I could have an extension.'”
  - Formality: “Tone is direct but acceptable for a follow-up request.”
  - Cultural: “In U.S. office hours, proposing a specific deadline (Friday) strengthens your request.”
- Click “Why?” on the cultural note to expand the rationale.

**Screen 5: Message Rewrite Mode**
- Paste a WhatsApp group message: “You didn’t do your part. What’s going on?”
- Select tone: “Softer / more diplomatic.”
- Show revised version with highlighted changes and a cultural note.

---

## 1:50–2:35 — Responsible AI

**Visual:** Quick cuts of three moments.

1. **Bias / Fairness:** Mei selects “Indian English.” The feedback says: “'Prepone' is understood in Indian English; in U.S. academic settings, 'move up' is more widely recognized.”
   - **Voiceover:** “We respect World Englishes. The tool doesn’t force a single standard.”

2. **Transparency:** Click “Why?” on a formality suggestion.
   - **Voiceover:** “Every suggestion explains its reasoning. Students learn *why*, not just *what*.”

3. **User Control:** Mei dismisses a feedback item.
   - **Voiceover:** “Students are the final author. They can edit or reject any suggestion.”

---

## 2:35–3:00 — Impact & Closing

**Visual:** Split screen of diverse students in classrooms; team photo with locations.

**Voiceover:**
> “LinguaLab turns anxiety into confidence. It’s a low-pressure practice space where every student can be heard—not just in their writing, but in the conversations that shape their academic future.”

**Final frame:**
- App logo + tagline: **“Your Voice, Across Cultures.”**
- Live demo URL + hackathon logo.

---

## Demo Checklist Before Recording

- [ ] Tested on both desktop and mobile screen sizes.
- [ ] Office Hours scenario flows without errors.
- [ ] Feedback panel renders correctly.
- [ ] “Why?” expansions work.
- [ ] Message Rewrite mode produces a good example.
- [ ] Voiceover fits within 3 minutes at normal pace.
- [ ] Background music is subtle or absent.
